Child Protection Policy

Last Updated: January 2025 | Review Date: January 2026

Shri Krishna Udhav Memorial Trust is committed to creating a safe environment for all children. This policy outlines our commitment to safeguarding children from harm.

1. Policy Statement

Shri Krishna Udhav Memorial Trust believes that every child has the right to protection from all forms of harm, abuse, neglect, and exploitation. We are committed to:

  • Creating a safe and nurturing environment for children in all our programs
  • Protecting children from physical, sexual, emotional abuse, and neglect
  • Promoting the welfare and best interests of all children we serve
  • Respecting the dignity, privacy, and rights of every child

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All trustees, employees, volunteers, and representatives of the trust
  • All children (under 18 years) who participate in our programs or activities
  • All partner organizations, contractors, and visitors engaging with children through our programs

3. Principles

  • Best Interests of the Child: All decisions and actions concerning children shall prioritize their best interests.
  • Non-Discrimination: Every child has equal rights to protection regardless of gender, caste, religion, disability, or socioeconomic status.
  • Participation: Children have the right to express their views and participate in decisions affecting them.
  • Confidentiality: Information about children and their families is handled with strict confidentiality.

4. Code of Conduct

All staff, volunteers, and representatives must:

  • Treat all children with respect and dignity
  • Never use physical punishment or abusive language
  • Avoid being alone with a child in private spaces
  • Not engage in any form of inappropriate physical contact
  • Not share personal contact information with children
  • Report any concerns about child safety immediately
  • Use appropriate language and behavior at all times

5. Recruitment & Screening

We implement thorough screening procedures for all staff and volunteers working with children:

  • Verification of identity and address
  • Reference checks from previous employers or organizations
  • Police verification where required by law
  • Signed declaration of compliance with child protection policies
  • Mandatory child protection training before engagement

6. Reporting Concerns

Anyone who suspects or witnesses child abuse or harmful behavior must report it immediately. Reports can be made to:

  • Email: krishnaudhavtrust@gmail.com

All reports will be taken seriously, investigated promptly, and handled with strict confidentiality.

7. Photography & Media

  • Written consent from parents/guardians is obtained before photographing or filming children
  • Children are never identified by full name in public materials
  • Images are used only for organizational purposes and not shared with third parties
  • Children have the right to refuse being photographed

8. Program Safety

  • Risk assessments are conducted for all activities involving children
  • Adequate adult supervision is maintained (minimum 2 adults)
  • First aid kits and emergency contacts are available at all program sites
  • Safe transportation arrangements are made when required
  • Program venues are inspected for safety hazards

9. Training & Awareness

All staff and volunteers undergo:

  • Induction training on child protection policies
  • Annual refresher training on recognizing and responding to abuse
  • Workshops on positive child engagement techniques
  • Awareness sessions on POCSO Act, 2012 and JJ Act

10. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually or sooner if required by changes in legislation or organizational needs. All updates are communicated to staff, volunteers, and relevant stakeholders.
